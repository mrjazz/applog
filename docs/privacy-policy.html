Privacy Policy

Axion Applog for macOS — last updated July 7, 2026

Summary: Axion Applog does not collect, transmit, or sell any of your data. Everything it records stays in a database file on your own Mac. There is no account, no analytics, no advertising, and no network connection of any kind.

What Axion Applog does

Axion Applog is a menu bar app that automatically observes which app, document, tab, or website is frontmost on your Mac, and for how long, so you can review your own usage patterns later. It runs entirely on-device.

What data is recorded

  • The bundle identifier and display name of the app in front
  • The title of the focused window, and — for supported browsers — the active tab's URL and domain
  • Timestamps and durations of the above, including idle and "away" periods
  • Aggregate keyboard/mouse activity counts (whether input occurred, never the content of what was typed or clicked)
  • Any tags, exclusions, or notes you choose to add

Axion Applog never records keystroke content, clipboard contents, screenshots, or the contents of any document, message, or page — only titles, URLs, and timing.

Where it's stored

All recorded data lives in a local SQLite database at ~/Library/Application Support/Axion Applog/ on your Mac. There is no cloud account, no remote server, and no third-party service involved in storing this data. Rolling local backups are kept for recovery purposes only, on the same disk.

What leaves your device

Nothing. Axion Applog has no network component. It does not transmit usage data, crash reports, or telemetry anywhere, and it does not share data with any third party, advertiser, or analytics provider.

Data only ever leaves your device if you choose to export it — as an HTML report, a CSV/JSON file, or a database file — and only to a location you pick yourself.

Permissions Axion Applog requests

  • Accessibility — used to read the title of the currently focused window in other apps, so that time can be attributed to the correct document, tab, or conversation. Axion Applog explains this the first time it's needed and links directly to System Settings; it never reads keystrokes through this permission.
  • Automation (Apple Events) — used only to ask supported browsers (Safari, Chrome, Edge, Arc) for the URL of the active tab, so time can be grouped by website domain. Axion Applog does not control, modify, or interact with any app beyond reading this one piece of information.

Your control over the data

  • Exclude specific apps or browser domains from ever being tracked
  • Hide, merge, rename, or manually correct any recorded item
  • Export or delete the underlying database at any time — it's a plain file you own

Children's privacy

Axion Applog is a general productivity tool not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from children, on-device or otherwise.
