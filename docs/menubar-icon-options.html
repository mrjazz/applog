AppTracker · Menu Bar Icon Review

Twenty ways to sit in the menu bar

AppTracker lives as a single glyph at 17pt, template-rendered so macOS can tint it for light and dark menu bars. Each candidate below is mounted at true size in both, next to the faint system glyphs it will actually sit beside, so shape and weight can be judged the way they'll really be seen.

Time Watching Light Nature Abstract

Time

05 candidates
HOURGLASS — SETTLED
Sand at rest in the lower bulb. Reads as "tracking," calm rather than urgent.
HOURGLASS — MID-FLOW
A grain caught mid-fall. More "live," but the loose dot can smear at 17pt.
CLOCK — TEN PAST TEN
The most literal read. Safe, but visually close to the system clock widget.
STOPWATCH
Crown at top-right breaks symmetry — reads as "measuring," a fraction more active than a clock.
SUNDIAL
Time as a passive shadow rather than a mechanism — quietest of the five, but unfamiliar at a glance.

Watching

05 candidates
LIGHTHOUSE
Watches quietly, signals when it matters. The most on-brand metaphor for passive tracking.
EYE
Unambiguous "this is watching you" — accurate to what the app does, maybe too on the nose.
RADAR SWEEP
Implies scanning/detection rather than recording. Slightly technical, matches a utility app.
BINOCULARS
Reads well large, but the twin barrels fight for pixels at 17pt and blur together.
BEACON SIGNAL
Borrowed from signal-strength language — "broadcasting quietly." Reads as status, not data.

Light

05 candidates
DESK LAMP
Focus/work-session framing rather than time. The angled arm is the app's most distinct silhouette.
LIGHT BULB
Instantly familiar as "idea/on," but heavily used elsewhere — least distinctive of this set.
CANDLE
A slower, ambient kind of time passing. Quiet and warm, if a little precious for a dev tool.
FLAME
"Active session" energy — closer to a streak or a build indicator than a passive tracker.
SPOTLIGHT CONE
"Shining a light on where your time goes" — the widening cone reads as attention, not measurement.

Nature

03 candidates
LEAF
Growth-over-time framing — gentle, but has no obvious link to "menu bar utility" without context.
CRESCENT MOON
Doubles as the natural glyph for the app's own "Away/idle" state — same icon, two jobs.
FOOTPRINTS
"Where you've been today" — a nice narrative fit, but reads as ambiguous dots at 17pt.

Abstract

02 candidates
PULSE LINE
Borrowed from health-monitor language — "activity is alive." Busiest shape of the set at small size.
TARGET
Perfectly symmetric and legible even at tiny sizes, but says "goal," not "time" — a slight meaning mismatch.
Rendered as template glyphs (single-color, currentColor) so each behaves like a real macOS menu bar icon — automatically dark-on-light and light-on-dark. Sizing shown at 17pt, the actual render size for a 22pt-tall menu bar.
